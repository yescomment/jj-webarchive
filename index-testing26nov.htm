The James Joyce Society, founded in 1947, is devoted to the appreciation of the life, works, and significance of the Irish author (1882-1941). Meetings, now virtual, originally took place several times a year at the Gotham Book Mart, a landmark bookstore and writers' center, at 41 West 47th Street in New York City...[more]

Programs: The Past schedule included presentations on Joyce and Homer, old Irish myth, Cloacal Aesthetics, Pretension & Failure ....[more] Membership: Print out the application form to join or renew The James Joyce Society for [2021 [more]

Archive: Past events in in archive included Bloomsday celebrations and programs on Vladimir Dixon and on Joyce and Homer....[more] Links: Follow links to the Finnegans Wake Society of New York and selected Joyce web pages for text, criticism, media, and discussion....[more]
