    With the end of additional copyright restrictions on original Joyce publications, more of his texts have become available in recent years than ever before for downloading and research. In addition, a most welcome resource for Joyce research is Hathitrust.org, a comsortium of Google, Internet Archive and other participants, which grew from 2 million total searchable pages in 2008 to 15 million pages in 2018, including more than two dozen links to historical Joyce editions. Moving from the macro to the micro, James Joyce Online Notes at jjon.org is a welcome recent entry. The bad news is that too many Joyce sites have diappeared, moved, or changed their focus from research to help for students writing papers. (Links last checked March 25, 2021.)

  • IJJF: James Joyce Resource Center at Tulsa The home page of the International James Joyce Foundation, devoted to encouraging scholarship, criticism and study of the life, work and career of the writer, with deep resources, symposium information and a web based newsgroup, led by a distinguished board of trustees, contains links, addresses, mailing lists, a timeline, and Joyce works, with a bibliography on Joyce and critical approaches to Joyce studies.
  • archive.org. The Internet Archive is a huge collectiom of downloadable full text James Joyce items, plus audio, movie, and image pages, each organized by type and by year. In addition, the Internet Archive hosts the Wayback Machine at web.archive.org/, a collection of 514 billion searchable historical web page snapshots.
  • hathitrust.org. Hathiturst, a consortium of Google, Internet Archive, Microsoft, and other participats, promises hundreds of searchable James Joyce titles. Particpating institutions grant individual researchers greater downloading access.
  • books.google.com.> A search in Google promises a huge number of Joyce titles. The contents of some titles even still in copyright may be "searched inside the book."

  • JJQ Wordpress blog : http://jjqblog.wordpress.com/ Notices and news of interest from James Joyce Quarterly.

  • James Joyce Online Notes: http://www.jjon.org/. A new "forum for the publication of documentary evidence"

  • The James Joyce Checklist: http://research.hrc.utexas.edu/jamesjoycechecklist/index.cfm A bIbliography of primary and secondary materials

  • Featured Author: James Joyce - http://www.nytimes.com/books/00/01/09/specials/joyce.html
    Reviews and articles on Joyce in the New York Times from 1919 to the present (requires login).
