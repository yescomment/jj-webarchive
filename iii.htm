Zack Bowen's recollections: Early days of The James Joyce Society
The James Joyce Society, founded in 1947, is devoted to the appreciation of the life, works, and significance of the Irish author (1882-1941). Meetings for many years took place at the legendary Gotham Book Mart, a landmark bookstore and writers' center, at 41 West 47th Street in New York City, and after it closed, at Glucksman Ireland House, NYU. and other locations. Currently meetings are conducted virtually. //
Past Programs have included presentations on Joyce and Homer, old Irish myth, Vladimir Dixon, Cloacal Aesthetics, Pretension and Failure, and Bloomsday celebrations. Membership information: Application form to join or renew membership in The James Joyce Society.
